# Internal honesty before external argument

## Statement

Before defending a position in a meeting, in a PR, in a conflict —
check internally: do you actually believe this, or are you defending
sunk cost / status / a position you adopted for the wrong reason? If
you can't pass that internal check honestly, soften the external
position to match.

## Why hold this

External force without internal honesty is performance. People feel
the gap, even when they can't articulate it. Over time, performing
conviction you don't have erodes both your authority and your own
sense of what you actually think. The internal check is the cheap
move that prevents the expensive drift.

## Edge cases — when not to apply

- Tactical advocacy where you're explicitly arguing one side as a
  device (devil's advocate, structured debate). The honesty applies
  to the meta-level: you're being honest that you're playing a role.
- Time-critical moments where internal reflection is a luxury — make
  the call, audit the conviction afterwards.
