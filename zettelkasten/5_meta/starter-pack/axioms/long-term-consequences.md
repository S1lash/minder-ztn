# Long-term consequences over short-term convenience

## Statement

Decisions outlive the moment they're made. Before committing to a
choice that will persist, model the people who will live with it next
year — not the people in the room today — and let their experience
weigh more than present convenience.

## Why hold this

Present-you has more context than future-you will. Future-them has
less context than present-you does. The information asymmetry is
permanent. The honest move is to leave the decision in a state that
the people inheriting it can understand, audit, and undo if needed.

## Edge cases — when not to apply

- Genuinely reversible decisions — those have a different cost
  function. Don't paralyze yourself over choices that take five
  minutes to undo.
- Acute time pressure where short-term survival outranks long-term
  optimization. Survive first, optimize later — but don't accidentally
  let "acute" become permanent.

## Edit me

The 12-24 month horizon is a starting heuristic. For some domains
(infrastructure, contracts) it's too short; for others (R&D, content)
it's too long. Adjust to the half-life of decisions in your own work.
