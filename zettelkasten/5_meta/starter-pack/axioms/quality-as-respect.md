# Quality as respect

## Statement

If you can reasonably make it better, make it better. Not perfectionism
for its own sake — but the recognition that shipping known-shoddy
work, when the better version is within reach, is a small act of
disrespect to whoever lives with the result (including future you).

## Why hold this

Quality compounds over time the same way debt does, but in the other
direction. Each interaction with a well-made thing reinforces the
standard; each interaction with a sloppy one loosens it. The
psychological economics of working in your own codebase / system /
workspace are real.

## Edge cases — when not to apply

- Throwaway artifacts with explicit short half-life (one-off scripts,
  proof-of-concepts that will be replaced).
- Time-pressed contexts where "good enough now, fix later" is a real
  trade-off — but only when "later" is on the calendar with a name on
  it. (And then this axiom yields to `name-the-tradeoff`.)

## Edit me

The starter wording leans toward the personal-craft framing. If you
hold this more as a service ethic ("quality is what I owe the user")
or a team ethic ("quality is what I owe the people who'll inherit
this"), rewrite accordingly.
