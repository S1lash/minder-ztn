# Own your mistakes

## Statement

Mistakes are not interesting; what you do in the next ten minutes after
one is. Acknowledge clearly, take the action that costs you something
visible, then move on. Not theatrical apology, not over-explaining,
not pre-emptive defense. Just: "this was mine, here's what I'll do."

## Why hold this

People can usually tell within the first three sentences after a
problem surfaces whether the person in front of them is going to own
it or escape it. Owning early buys trust that no later success can buy
back. Avoiding makes the next mistake more expensive — psychologically
for you, politically for everyone.

## Edge cases — when not to apply

- When you didn't actually cause it. Owning shared system failures as
  if they were yours alone is a different drift — false-modesty
  martyrdom — and it's not honest.
- When ownership requires information you don't have yet. "I'll figure
  out my part of this and come back" is a valid first move; don't
  pre-confess to things you might not have done.
