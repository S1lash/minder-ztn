# What doesn't count (opportunity)

- **Role-routine — a contact, event, OR fork explained entirely by the current role**,
  regardless of size or novelty. A six-figure deal, a go-live, a `(NEW)`-tagged cross-team
  counterpart, a delivery architecture choice — ordinary work. Test: "is its presence in the
  week explained entirely by the role, with no unplanned goal-relevant surface?" Yes → routine.
- **External validation as an open door** — praise, a declined invite, an ego/confidence
  signal — is NOT a door unless a concrete convertible next-action exists. Validation feeds
  confidence, not the portfolio.
- **A branch or tie without a one-week cheap test** — research bait. If it can't be reduced to
  a bird-in-hand move, drop it. (This keeps "new" from meaning "new rabbit hole" — the point of
  leading with new opportunities without a suppression gate.)
- **A branch with unbounded downside** dressed as opportunity — a good option is asymmetry
  (bounded downside, open upside), not "more stuff".
- **Apophenia** — an opportunity-person every single week is noise; 3+ stretched serendipities
  in a run. Most weeks: no genuine tie. "No new tie this week" is expected.
- **Instrumentalizing a person** — if a tie is expressible only as an extraction move ("use X
  for Y", "get friends to wear the device for distribution"), drop it. An artifact whose
  *purpose* is a named individual is the ick side; an artifact you'd build anyway is clean. The
  relationship is primary; the opportunity is a by-product the lens notices, never a purpose it
  assigns.
- **Abduction as fact** — a compelling "hidden profit" hypothesis without the "проверь" tag.
- **Re-pitching an old idea-note as a new opportunity** — old doors live in the change-layer as
  a delta, not as a fresh door (unless materially changed this week, then explicitly "raised as
  newly relevant"). "Investor lead — still open, waits until August, no movement this week"
  (delta), not "Investor lead — met via friend, wow-effect, funnel model, access to $1M…"
  (re-pitch).
- **A forced fork / manufactured urgency** — no binding course-choice this week is valid. A
  delivery-role decision is not the owner's course-fork.
- **Assumption-accuracy of a formal decision** — that is `decision-review`; here only
  action-fate (shipped / decayed).
- **A brooding loop** — that is `stalled-thread`, not an open opportunity node.
- **A concept↔concept structural bridge** — that is `cross-domain-bridge`.
- **Jargon / context-free references in the output** — no service or academic terms, no bare
  "the thing we discussed" / "the person from X"; every item carries its own who/what/when so it
  reads cold. Decay is base-rate data, never reproach or coaching.
